# Kotakureo UDL-DHH Framework

A practical framework for Universal Design for Learning (UDL) tailored for Deaf and Hard of Hearing (DHH) students in New Zealand classrooms. This resource provides evidence-based strategies to support inclusive teaching and learning.

## What is UDL-DHH?

UDL-DHH integrates UDL principles with DHH-specific needs, emphasizing:
- **Bi-Literacy**: Bridging NZSL and English
- **Sensory Access**: Managing fatigue and physical environment
- **Multi-Modal Teaching**: Visual and auditory strategies
- **Student Agency**: Building independence and self-advocacy

## The 8 Domains

1. **Domain A: Bi-Literacy Bridge** - Visual grammar, shape coding, and language chaining
2. **Domain B: Sensory & Physical Access** - Fatigue management and visual attention cues
3. **Domain C: Multi-Modal Instruction** - Separation of modalities and information chunking
4. **Domain D: Explicit Teaching & Scaffolding** - Gradual release and error analysis
5. **Domain E: Mana Motuhake & Agency** - Self-advocacy and response systems
6. **Domain F: Symbolic & Mathematical Structure** - Visual models for math and science
7. **Domain G: Critical Thinking & Metalinguistics** - Code-switching and visualization
8. **Domain H: Whanaungatanga & Engagement** - Cultural identity and relationships
